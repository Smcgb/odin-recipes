Vegetarian Cincinatti Chili

Description

A modified version of Cincinatti Chili using meat replacement such as Beyond or Impossible products

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb of your preferred meat replacement
  • 2 TBSP soy sauce (temari for Gluten free)
  • 2 TBSP apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 Square of Dark chocolate
  • 1/4 cup chili powder
  • 1 medium yellow onion
  • 1 TBSP fresh garlic
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 12 all spice berries
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 28 oz can tomato puree
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • diced onion
  • shredded cheddar cheese
  • oyster crackers
  • 1 lb cooked spaghetti

Instructions

  1. Combine meat replacement, soy sauce, and vinegar in a bowl and let sit for 30 minutes
  2. Heat a large pot over medium heat and add the meat replacement mixture
  3. Break up the meat replacement and cook until browning. This isn't real meat and undercooking is fine as it will finish in the chili mix later
  4. Add the chocolate, chili powder, onion, garlic, paprika, cumin, cinnamon, all spice, salt, and cayenne pepper
  5. Cook for 2 minutes
  6. Add the tomato puree and vegetable broth
  7. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 1 hour
  8. Remove all spice berries
  9. Serve over spaghetti with diced onion, shredded cheddar cheese, and oyster crackers
